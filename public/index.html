411VM

Growing up with ambitions to be a skateboard photographer, Paul initially discovered a lot of music through a monthly skateboarding video magazine called 411VM. Launched in 1993, 411VM profiled skateboarders from the US and across the world, with the skaters choosing the music for their video sections.

Because it reflected the breadth of global skateboard culture rather than specific scenes or brands, the range of music was unusually broad and formative.

Alan Watts

Alan Watts (1915–1973) was one of the first writers and speakers to interpret Eastern philosophy for Western audiences.

Born near London, Watts discovered Buddhism at a young age before moving to the United States in 1938. He later became an Episcopal priest, relocated to Millbrook, New York, and authored The Wisdom of Insecurity, one of his most influential works.

From the 1950s onward, his radio talks and lectures gained national reach, and by the 1960s he had become an unlikely spiritual voice for the counterculture movement.

Ali Farka Touré

An eclectic mix of music was always present in Paul’s home growing up — from Weather Report and Crosby, Stills & Nash to Bobby Womack and Malian musician Ali Farka Touré.

The 2002 documentary A Visit to Ali Farka Touré captures the artist later in life, focusing on his humanitarian efforts in Mali and how his work increasingly centred on improving living conditions rather than recording and touring.

Touré’s commitment to community and place offers a powerful example of how artistic purpose can evolve beyond commercial output.

John Coltrane — A Love Supreme

Growing up in Michigan, Zach Shigeto initially dreamed of becoming a professional jazz musician. A number of seminal jazz records formed the foundation of his musical education, including works by Pharoah Sanders, Miles Davis, Bill Evans, and Herbie Hancock.

Among these, John Coltrane’s A Love Supreme stood apart. Recorded in late 1964 and released on Impulse! Records in 1965, the album’s spiritual intensity and rhythmic depth left a lasting impression on Shigeto’s approach to music — both as a drummer and later as a producer.
